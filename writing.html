Why Offline Metrics Lie

An experiment in deceptive validation

Built a churn model that hit 0.92 AUC on holdout. Deployed it. Watched it fail spectacularly on month-2 data. Here's why stratified random splits hide temporal drift, and how I redesigned my eval suite to catch this before production.

Key takeaway: Time splits or you're lying to yourself.

What I'd do differently: Use time-based splits from day one. Never trust random holdout again. Add a "future simulation" test that holds out the most recent 20% by timestamp.
